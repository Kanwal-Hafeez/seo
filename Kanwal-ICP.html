The real picture

Where the Revenue Actually Goes

60,000 AED a month reads like a healthy clinic. In practice, rent and the day-to-day cost of running the place take their share before Dr. Ahmed ever sees a dirham of profit.

Monthly Revenue AED 60,000
Shop Rent ~AED 23,000 · 38%
Staff Salaries ~AED 18,000 · 30%
Equipment & Maintenance ~AED 15,000 · 25%
What's actually left over is thin, maybe AED 3,000-4,000 in an okay month, and some months, after a slow week or an unexpected repair bill, close to nothing at all.

This is a rough picture, not a real budget, we're not itemizing every cost. The point is simple: a clinic that looks like it's pulling in 60,000 AED a month can still leave its owner with barely anything to show for it.

Reading between the lines

What This Means for Outreach

Revenue-rich, margin-poor. The top line looks fine. What keeps him up is what's left after rent, equipment, and payroll clear.

Time-poor, not idea-poor. He's in the chair treating patients all day, not behind a desk running campaigns.

Once bitten. His own words draw a line between "dentist" and "marketing expert," the language of someone overpromised to before.

Buys outcomes, not services. He doesn't want a plan on a slide. He wants fewer empty chairs next month.
